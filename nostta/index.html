Game Design Document (GDD)

Game Title:

Evolved Hunt

1. Game Overview

1.1 Genre:
Action-Adventure with Survival Elements

1.2 Platforms:
PC (Windows), Console

1.3 Target Audience:
Ages 15+ (Fans of survival, action, and skill-based gameplay)

1.4 Unique Selling Proposition (USP):
Dynamic monster behavior creating tense and strategic gameplay.
Seamless integration of bow-and-arrow mechanics with visual trajectory lines.
Atmospheric environments, enhanced by adaptive lighting and immersive sound design.



2. Story and Setting

2.1 Premise:
The player is a lone wanderer in a post-apocalyptic world teeming with evolved creatures. These creatures, each with unique behaviors and lethal abilities, dominate the land. Armed with a bow and a limited set of resources, the player must navigate perilous environments, uncover the mysteries of this world, and survive relentless adversaries.


2.2 Setting:
Biomes: Dense forests, abandoned ruins, underground caves, and misty mountains.
Atmosphere: A blend of eerie silence and dynamic tension, punctuated by creature encounters.

2.3 Narrative:
As the player progresses, they uncover fragmented lore about the evolution of the creatures and the catastrophic events that led to humanity's fall.



3. Gameplay Mechanics

3.1 Core Loop:
Explore: Traverse diverse environments, find resources, and navigate hidden dangers.

Survive: Evade and outwit creatures with unique attack patterns and behaviors.

Engage: Use the bow and arrow with precision to fend off threats.

Progress: Solve environmental puzzles and upgrade equipment to unlock new areas.



3.2 Key Mechanics:

Player Movement
Smooth transitions with environment-specific behaviors (e.g., slowing in water, climbing steep surfaces).
Responsive controls for quick dodges and jumps during combat.

Bow and Arrow System
Visual trajectory line for precise aiming and strategic shots.
Arrows can interact with the environment (e.g., lighting torches, triggering traps).

Dynamic Camera System

Cinemachine Virtual Camera tracks the player dynamically, providing cinematic angles during pivotal moments.
Zooms out during exploration and tightens focus during combat.

Villain Behaviors

The Evolved Spider:
Unrelenting, unkillable threat requiring constant evasion.
Tracks the player with a poisonous sting and follows movements precisely.

The Evolved Crab:
Stealthy and lethal, becoming visible only when close.
Latches onto players, immobilizing them instantly. Can be countered only with quick reflexes and accurate bow shots.

The Flying Evolved Monster:
Aerial predator with erratic movement.
Executes dual-phase attacks requiring vigilant dodging and precise shooting to defeat.

UI/UX and HUD
Minimalist HUD showing health, potion counters, and arrow inventory.
Context-sensitive indicators (e.g., warnings for nearby threats or objectives).



4. Visual and Audio Design

4.1 Lighting and Environment Design
Dynamic lighting adapts to the player’s progress (e.g., flickering torches, glowing bioluminescent plants in caves).
Environments are designed to guide players subtly through visual cues like light and color.

4.2 Sound Design
Environmental audio effects, such as rustling leaves or dripping water, set the tone.
Creature-specific sounds build tension (e.g., clicking of the Spider’s legs or the Crab’s scuttling).
Satisfying feedback for actions like shooting arrows or using potions.

4.3 Post-Processing Effects
Bloom, depth of field, and color grading enhance immersion.
Subtle motion blur and vignette effects intensify high-action sequences.




5. Progression and Systems

5.1 Progression:
Unlock new areas and abilities as the player solves puzzles and collects rare resources.
Skill upgrades include faster movement, increased arrow precision, and crafting unique arrow types.

5.2 Resource Management:
Limited arrows and health potions encourage strategic use.
Collect materials to craft arrows and upgrade the bow.




6. Optimization and Performance

6.1 Optimization with Occlusion Culling:
Only visible elements are rendered, ensuring smooth performance on low-end systems.

6.2 AI Optimization:
Adaptive AI systems ensure creatures behave dynamically without overwhelming system resources.




7. Narrative and Themes

7.1 Themes:
Survival against overwhelming odds.
The consequences of human actions on nature.
Resilience and adaptation in a hostile world.

7.2 Lore Delivery:
Environmental storytelling through abandoned structures, murals, and artifacts.
Audio logs or journals that reveal pieces of the past.




8. Monetization and Expansions

8.1 Monetization Model:
Base game with potential for expansions or DLC (e.g., new creatures, areas, or weapons).


8.2 Expansions:
DLC 1: New biome with volcanic terrain and lava-themed evolved creatures.
DLC 2: Additional lore, a new companion system, and advanced bow upgrades.
